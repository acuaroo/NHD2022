Coal between the 1850s up until the 1950s was absolutely essential, and was the highest consumed energy source for America at the time. Coal was one of the major foundations of industrialization throughout the 19th century and its use over petroleum stayed higher by around 10~13 QBTU (Quadrillion British thermal units) through the period highlighted in red. Coal's drop suffered at around 1940 was due to a massive coal miner strike with 500,000 miners in April 1943 due to unfair conditions despite President Roosevelt's attempts to make miners adhere to a “ no-strike” period during the Second World War. Nonetheless, coal was still vital

With a rising necessity for coal in the US, that comes with the rising need for mines, and of course, workers. Colorado Fuel & Iron company (or CF&I), owned by John D. Rockefeller Junior, set up their establishments in Denver and Pueblo in 1892. Their practices, however, were questionable under the Colorado law at the time. They purposely hired as many foreign workers as they were able to, as in the companies eyes, if they couldn't speak the same language, it would be harder for them to find a common goal and unite together. The blatant mine saftey violations that were occuring was most likely a factor contributing to Colorado having twice the national average of coal mine fatalities.
